Introduction

The Indian Defence Forces are the combined military forces of India, consisting of three major branches: the Indian Army, the Indian Navy, and the Indian Air Force. These forces are responsible for safeguarding the nation's sovereignty and security from external and internal threats.

Indian Army

The Indian Army is the land-based branch of the Indian Armed Forces. It plays a vital role in ensuring national security and defending the country's borders against external aggression and internal threats.

Indian Navy

The Indian Navy is the maritime branch of the Indian Armed Forces. Its primary objective is to protect India's maritime interests, secure its coastlines, and conduct humanitarian missions and disaster relief.

Indian Air Force

The Indian Air Force (IAF) is the air warfare and aerial defense branch of the Indian Armed Forces. It plays a key role in securing Indian airspace and providing support during ground operations.
